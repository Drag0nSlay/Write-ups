# Analysis of Suspected Inauthentic X Account (@Shikhasinghana)

## 1. Executive Summary

An X (Twitter) account operating under the identity "Shikha Singh" 
was analyzed using open-source intelligence (OSINT) techniques.

The investigation identified inconsistencies in linguistic patterns, 
content themes, temporal activity, and platform metadata.

Key indicators include identity shifts over time, non-standard language usage, 
and discrepancies between claimed identity and observed behavior.

The account engaged in narratives targeting Indian institutions, including 
the Indian Army, as well as political entities (BJP) and religious groups 
(Hindus).

At the time of final review, the account was observed to be unavailable 
on the X platform.

The findings indicate a high likelihood of identity misrepresentation 
and behavior consistent with inauthentic or coordinated activity.

## 2. Objectives

- Assess authenticity of the account identity  
- Identify linguistic, behavioral, and metadata anomalies  
- Analyze narrative intent and activity patterns

## 3. Methodology

1. Profile and metadata analysis  
2. Linguistic and content evaluation  
3. Timeline and posting behavior analysis  
4. Media (image/video) verification  
5. Interaction and engagement pattern review

## 4. Investigation Findings
### 4.1 Profile & Identity Signals

- Account presents as an Indian Hindu female identity
- Profile image introduced later (May 2024), inconsistent with earlier content  
- No continuous identity consistency across account timeline

### 4.2 Platform Metadata Analysis (Critical Evidence)

- **Account creation date:** September 2020  
- Verified status obtained in December 2025  

- **Username changes:** 27  
  - Significantly higher than normal user behavior  
  - Indicates repeated identity modification or rebranding  

- **Platform-indicated location:** “West Asia”  
  - Does not align with claimed Indian identity  
  - Suggests geographic inconsistency  

These indicators reflect identity instability and strengthen suspicion 
of inauthentic behavior.

### 4.3 Linguistic Analysis

- Use of Urdu-influenced phrases such as “Inshallah”  
- Presence of non-standard Hindi punctuation (e.g., “।।”)  
- Irregular spacing patterns between Hindi words  

These patterns suggest inconsistent or non-native linguistic behavior.

### 4.4 Content & Narrative Targeting

- Repeated criticism of Indian Army and national institutions  
- Content targeting BJP, RSS, and Hindu religious sentiments  
- Alignment with geopolitical narratives (e.g., Iran–Israel topics)  
- Instances of misleading or misidentified media (e.g., incorrect video attribution)

### 4.5 Behavioural Patterns

- Long-term thematic consistency across posts  
- Engagement in emotionally charged and polarizing discussions  
- Interaction with accounts sharing similar narratives

### 4.6 Temporal Analysis

- Posting activity aligned with Ramadan-related time periods  
- Repeated activity during pre-dawn and evening intervals  
- Timing patterns not fully consistent with typical Indian usage behavior

### 4.7 Media & Profile Image Analysis

- Profile image appears inconsistent with earlier account behavior  
- No verifiable ownership of profile image identified 
- Possible use of externally sourced or non-authentic imagery

## 5. Attribution Assessment

**Claimed Identity:** Indian Hindu female (unverified)  
**Observed Behavior:** Inconsistent with claimed identity  
**Location Indicator:** Broad regional mismatch (West Asia)  
**Behavior Type:** Suspected Inauthentic or Coordinated Activity  

**Confidence in Identity Misrepresentation:** High  
**Confidence in Geographic Attribution:** Moderate  
**Confidence in Individual Attribution:** Low

## 6. Risk Assessment

- Amplification of politically and religiously sensitive narratives  
- Potential influence on perception of Indian institutions  
- Contribution to misinformation and social polarization

## 7. Conclusion

The account demonstrates multiple indicators of identity inconsistency, 
including linguistic anomalies, metadata irregularities, and behavioral patterns.

The observed activity includes targeted narratives against Indian institutions, 
political entities (BJP), and religious groups (Hindus).

The presence of frequent username changes, delayed verification, and regional 
metadata mismatch further strengthens the likelihood of identity manipulation.

At the time of analysis, the account was unavailable on the X platform. 
The exact cause of removal cannot be independently verified.

## 8. Analytical Insights

This case reflects a pattern where accounts are created, aged, modified, 
and later used for narrative amplification.

Such accounts may gradually build credibility before engaging in targeted 
content designed to influence public perception.

## Ethical Considerations

This investigation is based solely on publicly available information.

No intrusive methods were used, and no unverified personal identity 
has been presented as confirmed fact.
