# Analysis of Suspected Inauthentic X Account & Narrative Targeting

## 1. Executive Summary

An X (Twitter) account claiming to represent an Indian individual was analyzed using open-source intelligence techniques.

Multiple inconsistencies were identified between the claimed persona and observable indicators, including platform metadata, content patterns, posting behavior, and media verification.

The account consistently publishes content targeting Indian political entities, including BJP and Prime Minister Narendra Modi, as well as religious groups, particularly Hindus, using emotionally charged narratives.

Findings indicate a high likelihood of identity misrepresentation and behavior consistent with coordinated inauthentic activity. Attribution to a specific individual cannot be conclusively established using OSINT alone.

## 2. Objective

- Verify authenticity of the claimed identity.
- Analyze behavioral and content patterns.
- Identify indicators of coordinated or deceptive activity.
- Assess potential influence and risk.

## 7. Conclusion

The account exhibits multiple indicators of identity misrepresentation and coordinated inauthentic behavior.

Content analysis confirms consistent targeting of Indian political actors, including BJP and Prime Minister Narendra Modi, as well as religious groups such as Hindus, through emotionally driven narratives.

While definitive attribution to a specific operator is not possible using OSINT alone, the overall evidence strongly suggests the account does not authentically represent its claimed identity.

## 8. Analytical Insight

This case reflects a broader pattern in influence operations where fabricated or misleading identities are used to gain credibility within a target audience and amplify divisive narratives.
